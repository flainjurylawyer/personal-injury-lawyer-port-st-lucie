Understanding Compensation in Personal Injury Cases

Victims of personal injury accidents in Port St. Lucie may be entitled to various types of compensation. The damages you can recover generally fall into two categories: economic and non-economic.

Economic Damages

These damages cover the tangible financial losses you’ve incurred as a result of your injury, such as:

  • Medical Expenses: Includes hospital bills, surgery costs, physical therapy, and ongoing medical care.
  • Lost Wages: Reimbursement for income lost due to your inability to work.
  • Property Damage: Covers the cost to repair or replace damaged property, such as your vehicle.

Non-Economic Damages

Non-economic damages address the intangible losses associated with your injury, including:

  • Pain and Suffering: Compensation for physical pain and emotional trauma.
  • Loss of Enjoyment of Life: Damages for reduced quality of life due to your injuries.
  • Emotional Distress: Covers psychological impacts like anxiety, depression, or PTSD.

The Importance of Acting Quickly

In Florida, there are strict deadlines for filing personal injury claims. The statute of limitations for most personal injury cases is four years from the date of the accident. Missing this deadline could result in losing your right to seek compensation. However, certain circumstances, such as cases involving government entities, may have shorter deadlines. This makes it crucial to consult with a Port St. Lucie personal injury lawyer as soon as possible.
